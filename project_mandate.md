# Documentation: Project Mandate
**Version:** 1.2

## 1. Project Overview

* **Project Name:** "Bóora!"
* **Domain:** Multi-tenant Hyperlocal Experience Platform (TravelTech / Local Commerce)
* **High-Level Goal:** To establish a multilateral hyperlocal platform that connects residents, tourists, and local cultural/commercial operators through discovery, social mobilization, and transaction of local experiences, events, and products.
* **Platform Thesis:** Bóora! is not a one-sided city guide or a passive listing catalog. It is a multilateral destination platform that must simultaneously solve discovery/mobilization for people and visibility/revenue activation for the local trade cultural.
* **Replication Posture:** The product core should remain reusable across destination tenants, allowing local branding, supply curation, and operating posture to vary without redefining the platform itself.

---

## 2. Core Business Principles

These principles define the purpose and ethical boundaries of the platform. They are the foundational "Why" and guide all business and engineering decisions.

* **P-B1: Local Empowerment First:** We aim to improve local businesses, not to replace them. The platform's success is measured by the success of the local operators and communities it serves.
* **P-B2: Additive Revenue Streams:** We want to give more streams of revenue to local businesses, not strip them from what they have or own their customer relationships exclusively.
* **P-B3: Authentic Connection:** The platform must foster genuine connections between people (moratoriums, tourists) and the local culture (specialists, producers), prioritizing authenticity over pure commoditization.
* **P-B4: Symbiotic Ecosystem:** The platform is not a zero-sum game. The relationship between users, local operators, and the platform must be mutually beneficial.
* **P-B5: Social Mobilization as North Star:** The definitive metric is real-world movement—`Convites Aceitos` + `Presenças Confirmadas`—tracked all-time and in the current month (“Em Alta”). Vanity metrics (followers, likes, stars) do not guide success.
* **P-B6: Privacy with Agency:** Users can operate publicly or limit full-profile visibility to viewers they explicitly approve through favorites while still participating in invites; reciprocal favorites become product-level friends, rankings anonymize private profiles (blur/avatar masking), and unilateral contact matches may receive only capped exposure.
* **P-B7: Seeded Liquidity Before Organic Marketplace:** A tenant may bootstrap value through curated supply, tenant-operated inventory, and unmanaged account profiles before local operators fully self-manage their presence. Cold-start activation is a valid recurring operating posture, not a product anomaly.

## 3. Social Pivot Summary

*We are no longer just a city guide; we are a social engine for each tenant city.* Discovery exists to feed action. The home experience prioritizes pending invites and confirmed agenda over generic listings; operator value is proven through mobilized presences, not star ratings. Every actor—users, Pro/Verificados, operator-facing profiles, curators, and the broader local trade/cultural network—shares the same profile substrate, with Pro/Verificado status unlocking monetization (spaces, apoiadores) and enhanced limits.
